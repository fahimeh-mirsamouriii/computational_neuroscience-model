
​Computational Neuroscience Toolbox 🧠 Basics to Dynamics
​Welcome to my repository! This project is a collection of computational models exploring the mathematical beauty of neural activity. As a Physics graduate with a passion for Computational Neuroscience, I’ve built these models to bridge the gap between biological observations and mathematical abstractions.
​📚 Theoretical Foundations
​The implementations in this repository are deeply inspired by two cornerstone resources:
​"Principles of Modeling Small Populations of Neurons" by Christoph Börgers: For the rigorous dynamical systems approach and numerical accuracy.
​"Neuroscience: Exploring the Brain" by Mark Bear: For the biological intuition and understanding of synaptic functions.
​🛠 What's Inside?
​This Jupyter Notebook serves as a step-by-step guide through the following concepts:
​1. Single Neuron Abstractions
​LIF (Leaky Integrate-and-Fire): The fundamental "RC circuit" model of a neuron.
​Izhikevich Model: A computationally efficient model capable of reproducing diverse biological firing patterns (Regular Spiking, Bursting, etc.).
​2. Synaptic Communication
​Alpha Function Conductance: Modeling the time-course of neurotransmitter release and its effect on post-synaptic potentials (EPSPs).
​3. The Geometry of Thinking (Dynamical Systems)
​FitzHugh-Nagumo (FHN) Analysis: A deep dive into the Phase Plane. Here, I visualize Nullclines and Vector Fields to explain why and how a neuron decides to "fire" from a topological perspective.
​4. Neural Signal Analysis
​Spectral Analysis (PSD): Using the Welch method to extract rhythmic oscillations (like Alpha and Gamma waves) from noisy neural data—a crucial skill for analyzing LFP or EEG signals.
